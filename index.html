Eight Decades of Change
Our History
The Vulcan Society

Named after the Roman god of fire and forge, the Vulcan Society has been breaking barriers and building paths for Black firefighters in New York City since 1940.

1940
Founding

A group of Black firefighters founded the Vulcan Society to address racial discrimination and advocate for equal opportunities within the FDNY. Named for the Roman god of fire, the organization set out to forge a more equitable future for all firefighters of color.

1940s70s
Breaking Barriers

Through persistent advocacy and internal organizing, the Vulcan Society fought discriminatory hiring and promotion practices. Members rose through the ranks, demonstrating that excellence knows no color and paving the way for future generations.

1980s90s
Growing the Movement

The organization expanded its community outreach, establishing scholarship funds and mentorship initiatives for aspiring firefighters of color across New York City's five boroughs building the infrastructure for lasting change.

2007
Landmark Federal Victory

The Vulcan Society played a pivotal role in United States v. City of New York, a federal lawsuit challenging the FDNY's discriminatory hiring practices. The victory led to sweeping reforms in recruitment, hiring, and promotion across the department.

2010s
Reforming the FDNY

Working alongside city officials to implement court-mandated reforms, the Vulcan Society drove a significant increase in diversity across FDNY recruitment classes and leadership a tangible, measurable legacy of the legal victory.

Now
Continuing the Legacy

Today the Vulcan Society remains a powerful voice for justice within the fire service running annual scholarship dinners, fire safety workshops, and mentorship programs that impact thousands of New Yorkers every year.
